
The main thing is that we apply a backbone model, for example ResNet-50, and its input is the raw image. The raw image passes through ResNet-50, and from the early layer(example layer 2) of ResNet-50 we take the low-level feature map, which contains fine details like edges and boundaries. From the deeper/final layer of ResNet-50 we get the high-level feature map, which contains semantic information like object and class meaning.

The high-level feature map goes to ASPP. In ASPP, separate operations are applied in parallel: 1×1 Conv, 3×3 Atrous Conv rate 6, 3×3 Atrous Conv rate 12, 3×3 Atrous Conv rate 18, and Image Pooling. These separate outputs are then concatenated together, and after concatenation a 1×1 Projection Conv is applied. This produces the final encoder output feature map.

Then in the decoder, the low-level feature map from the early layer of ResNet-50 is taken, and a 1×1 Conv is applied to reduce its channels. At the same time, the final encoder output feature map is upsampled by 4. Then the processed low-level feature map and the upsampled encoder output are concatenated. After that, 3×3 Conv layers are applied to refine the combined features. Finally, the output is upsampled by 4 to produce the final segmentation output.
